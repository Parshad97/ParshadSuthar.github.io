About Me

Ph.D. Student in AI and Machine Learning

I'm excited to share that I'm currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Computer Science, specializing in AI and Machine Learning. My research journey has taken me through various domains, including Machine Learning, Deep Learning, OCR, and Graph Machine Learning. It's been an exhilarating experience exploring the world of applied AI and Machine Learning.

Presently, my work focuses on an MU-Coulter funded project dedicated to Deep Learning for the Early Detection of Dysplasia in IBD Patients. Additionally, I am involved in an NEH funded project on the CLIP model.

My core interests lie in computer vision, large language models (LLMs), Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Graph Neural Networks. I am passionate about delving into the intricacies of data and pushing the limits of AI to transform innovative concepts into reality.

When I'm not immersed in research, I love to travel, spend quality time with friends, play cricket, watch movies, listen to podcasts, and enjoy audiobooks.
